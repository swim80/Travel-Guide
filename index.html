Thessaloniki — The City

History, sea views, and the most vibrant street life in Greece

Landmark

White Tower & the Waterfront

The symbol of the city stands on a promenade that stretches for kilometres. Walk it at golden hour for views across the Thermaic Gulf that you won't forget.

Taste Thessaloniki

The city that takes its food more seriously than anywhere else in Greece

Bougatsa — Tasos

Fresh bougatsa sliced in front of you — crispy filo, warm custard, dusted with sugar. One of the most authentic spots in the city.

Find it

Trigona — Elenidis

The original and most famous trigona in the city. Crispy, syrupy cones filled with fresh cream. A Thessaloniki dessert you simply cannot skip.

Find it

Koulouri — Ouzounian

The Thessaloniki koulouri is in a different league. Ouzounian bakes them fresh every morning — sesame-crusted, chewy, utterly addictive for breakfast.

Find it

Dinner — Mamalouka

Modern Greek cuisine in a stylish setting. Creative mezedes, excellent natural wine, and a buzz that makes it the ideal dinner destination.

Find it
☀️

A note on eating here

Thessaloniki has a justified reputation as the food capital of Greece. Locals eat late, eat slowly, and eat a lot. Lunch after 2 pm, dinner after 9 pm. Follow their lead.

Experiences

Things to do beyond the beach — all well worth it

⛵

Rent a Boat in Vourvourou

No licence needed for smaller boats. Explore the islets, find a deserted beach, and have lunch on the water. One of the best half-days in Halkidiki.

Find rentals 🏔️

Mount Athos Cruise

Board a boat from Ouranoupoli for a coastal cruise past the monasteries of the Holy Mountain. You can't land without a permit, but the views from the water are extraordinary.

Departures from Ouranoupoli 🦞

Seaside Seafood

Pull up to a taverna by the water in Sithonia and order whatever the boat brought in that morning. Grilled octopus, fresh sea bream, cold ouzo.

Find a taverna

Optional Day Trips

A little history goes a long way out here

~1 hr drive

Vergina — Royal Tombs

One of the most significant archaeological finds of the 20th century. The tomb of Philip II of Macedon — Alexander the Great's father — with its treasures still intact. Genuinely breathtaking.

UNESCO site Philip II's tomb Well worth it

Near Halkidiki

Ancient Olynthos

Combine a beach day with a quick stop at this well-preserved Greek city destroyed by Philip II in 348 BC. The mosaic floors are still in place, and you'll almost certainly have the site to yourself.

Ancient mosaics Rarely crowded Easy combo with beach
